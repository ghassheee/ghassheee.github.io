# Trigonometric function ## Definition Euler's Formula $$ \begin{eqnarray} e^{-ix} &=& \cos{(-x)} + i \sin{(-x)} \\ &=& \cos{x} - i \sin{x} \end{eqnarray} $$ leads to $$ \begin{cases} e^{ix} + e^{-ix} = 2\cos{x} \\\ e^{ix} - e^{-ix} = 2i \sin{x} \end{cases} \Leftrightarrow \begin{cases} \cos{x} = \frac{1}{2}(e^{ix} + e^{-ix}) \\\ \sin{x} = \frac{1}{2i}(e^{ix} - e^{-ix}) \end{cases} \tag{3} $$ then just extend x $ \mapsto $ z .
This is the definition of $ \sin(z) $ , $ \cos(z) $ $$ \begin{eqnarray} \begin{cases} \cos{z} : &=& \frac{1}{2}(e^{iz} + e^{-iz}) \\\ \sin{z} : &=& \frac{1}{2i}(e^{iz} - e^{-iz}) \end{cases} \end{eqnarray} $$ ## visualization $ w = \cos{z} $ ![](/image/complex-trigonometric/complex-trigonometric-01.png width="600") $ w = \sin{z} $ ![](/image/complex-trigonometric/complex-trigonometric-02.png width="600") ## Euler's Formula in Complex Number
